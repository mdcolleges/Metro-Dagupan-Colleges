Courses

Criminology

Criminology offers a rich, multidisciplinary education that blends sociology, psychology, law, forensics, and policy studies to help you understand why crimes happen and how societies respond . It develops strong analytical and research skills

College Of Business Administration Tourism And Computer Sciences

The College of Business Administration, Tourism & Computer Science offers undergraduate degrees in Business Administration (e.g., Marketing, Finance, HR, Customs), Tourism and Hospitality Management, and Computer Science/IT (including BSCS, BSIT, and diplomas), blending theory with hands‑on training through specialized labs, professional software, internships, and real-world immersion to prepare graduates for careers in business, tourism, hospitality, software development, cybersecurity, and IT services

College Of Teachers Education

The College Of Teacher Education (CoTE) central administrative body overseeing educator licensure programs—coordinating curriculum development, clinical experiences, licensure applications, and state-level compliance to ensure high-quality preparation for future teachers, administrators, and school service professionals

Institututes Of Graduates

An institute is a specialized academic division within a college or university focused on a specific field—conducting research, offering targeted instruction, and often providing vocational training or certification—in contrast to a college, which typically offers broader undergraduate (and sometimes graduate) degree programs across various disciplines

Productivity High School

High school is a form of secondary education typically covering grades 9 through 12 (ages ~14–18) that follows elementary or junior high school. It prepares students for higher education or the workforce, offering core academic subjects alongside electives and extracurricular activities
